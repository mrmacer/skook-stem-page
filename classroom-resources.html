Resource Library

Grab-and-Go Materials
Built for Real Classrooms

Every resource in the IU29 library was developed with Schuylkill County classrooms in mind — not repurposed from somewhere else, not a vendor product, not theory. These are materials that teachers have actually used, refined, and taught with.

All materials are tagged to Pennsylvania STEELS standards, organized by subject area and grade band, and available at no cost to IU29 member district staff. The library grows every year — and you can contribute to it.

Library Contents

What You'll Find

Four types of classroom materials — each designed to save you planning time without sacrificing quality or alignment.

📄

Ready-to-Use Lesson Plans

Complete lesson plans with objectives, materials lists, step-by-step procedures, and STEELS standards documentation — ready the day you download them.

🔬

Lab & Design Challenges

Hands-on science investigations and engineering design challenges aligned to 3D STEELS — with student data sheets, teacher guides, and sample work.

📊

Assessment Tools

Formative and summative options — rubrics, checklists, claim-evidence-reasoning prompts, and computational thinking assessments designed for STEELS.

🏗️

Project-Based Learning Units

Multi-week PBL units with driving questions, anchor phenomena, and culminating tasks. Built for sustained inquiry — not just surface engagement.

Browse by Subject

Organized by STEELS Domain

Every resource is tagged to one or more STEELS domains — making it easy to find what fits your curriculum sequence.

🧬

Life Science

3.1 standards — K–12. Ecosystems, heredity, evolution, and biological systems. Includes cross-disciplinary lessons connecting life science to robotics and engineering.

⚗️

Physical Science

3.2 standards — K–12. Matter, energy, forces, and waves. Hands-on investigations and design challenges connecting chemistry and physics to real-world phenomena.

🌍

Earth & Space Science

3.3 standards — K–12. Earth's systems, climate, and place in the universe — including local watershed investigations and human impact studies.

🌱

Environmental Literacy

3.4 standards — K–12. Ecological thinking, environmental stewardship, and MWEE-aligned field experiences including the Shared Waters program.

⚙️

Technology & Engineering

3.5 + ETS standards — K–12. Engineering design challenges, robotics integration, K'Nex competitions, and Hummingbird-based T&E lessons across grade levels.

💻

Computer Science & Computational Thinking

Coding, robotics, and algorithm design — from Scratch and Hummingbird for younger students to computational thinking assessment frameworks for middle school.
