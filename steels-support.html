Pennsylvania Standards

A New Framework
for Science Education

Pennsylvania's STEELS standards represent the most significant shift in K–12 science education in decades. Built on three dimensions that work together — Science & Engineering Practices, Disciplinary Core Ideas, and Crosscutting Concepts — they ask teachers to teach differently, not just teach different content.

That's a lot to unpack. Whether you're a classroom teacher navigating new lessons or a curriculum director rebuilding your K–12 scope and sequence, IU29 provides practical, hands-on support that meets your district where it is — and helps it move forward.

Three-Dimensional Teaching

STEELS weaves Science & Engineering Practices, Disciplinary Core Ideas, and Crosscutting Concepts into lessons that build lasting understanding — not just content recall.

🌱

Environmental Literacy Built In

Unlike previous frameworks, STEELS explicitly integrates environmental literacy and sustainability — connecting science to the world students actually live in.

⚙️

Engineering as a Core Practice

Technology & Engineering isn't an add-on. STEELS positions engineering design as a central practice across all grade levels — supporting deeper problem-solving and real-world application.

Standards Framework

STEELS Standards Domains

Pennsylvania's STEELS framework spans six domains across all grade levels, K through 12.

🧬

3.1 — Life Science

Ecosystems, heredity, evolution, and the molecular basis of heredity — from kindergarten through AP-level biology.

⚗️

3.2 — Physical Science

Matter and its interactions, motion and stability, energy, and waves — connecting chemistry and physics to real phenomena.

🌍

3.3 — Earth & Space Science

Earth's systems, history, place in the universe, and climate — including human impacts and long-term sustainability.

🌱

3.4 — Environmental Literacy & Sustainability

A STEELS-first domain: connecting ecological thinking, environmental stewardship, and systems-level understanding across disciplines.

⚙️

3.5 — Technology & Engineering

The nature of technology, engineering design processes, and the interconnections between engineering, science, and society.

🏗️

ETS — Engineering Design

Problem scoping, solution testing, and iterative refinement — the engineering design process applied as a core science practice across all domains.

IU29 offers a range of professional learning formats — from intensive workshops to coaching cycles to regional networks — designed to build capacity over time, not just add knowledge in the moment.
